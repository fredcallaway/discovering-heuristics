We need your consent to proceed

This HIT is a psychology experiment being conducted by Fred Callaway and Jessica Hamrick, graduate students in Professor Tom Griffiths’s Computational Cognitive Science Lab at the University of California, Berkeley. In order to consent to participate, you MUST meet the following criteria:

  • 18 years of age or older.
  • Fluent speaker of English.
  • Have not participated in this experiment before.

This study is designed to investigate how people can make judgments about how physical objects and/or agents interact with each other. You will be asked to perform tasks that involve seeing objects and performing memory and/or problem solving tasks.

The study will take about 14 minutes, and will pay $1.50.

Your participation in this research is voluntary. You may refrain from answering any questions that make you uncomfortable and may withdraw your participation at any time without penalty by returning the HIT on MTurk and emailing the experimenter at the address provided below.

Participating will provide no direct benefits to you. But, we hope that this research will benefit society at large. Many problems that people can solve very easily are very difficult to automate (to have computers solve). By revealing more about how humans learn and think, our research may help others to build and program computers that can solve these kinds of problems.

Your MTurk worker ID may be connected to individual responses, and you may be asked to provide an email address, but this will be optional (you can complete the whole study without providing an email should you choose). We will handle responses as confidentially as possible. We cannot however guarantee the confidentiality of information transmitted over the Internet. We will be keeping data collected as part of this experiment indefinitely. Data without any personally identifiable data associated with it will be made available on public research data sharing sites for further analysis.

If you have any questions about the study, feel free to contact Fred Callaway. He can be reached at fredcallaway@berkeley.edu. If you have concerns about your rights as a participant in this experiment, please contact UC Berkeley’s Committee for Protection of Human Subjects at (510) 642-7461 or subjects@berkeley.edu.
